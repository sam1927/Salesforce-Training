      <h1>Switching Systems Mid-Month</h1>
      <p class="retro-content-subtitle">
        A short guide for moving from Google Sheets to Salesforce mid-month without affecting invoicing or payment.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div class="retro-stepper" data-stepper>

      <div class="retro-step-indicator">
        Step <span data-step-current>1</span> of <span data-step-total>8</span>
      </div>

      <div class="retro-step-stage">

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel is-active" data-step-panel>
          <h2>Overview</h2>
          <p>You will use two systems temporarily:</p>
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Google Sheets</strong> (old)</li>
            <li><strong>Salesforce</strong> (new)</li>
          </ul>
          <p><strong>Goal:</strong> make sure invoicing is correct.</p>
        </section>

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel" data-step-panel>
          <h2>Step 1: Choose Your Move Date</h2>
          <p>You can choose this date.</p>
          <p>Example: <strong>18th of the month</strong></p>
          <p>This is the date you start using <strong>Salesforce only</strong>.</p>
        </section>

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel" data-step-panel>
          <h2>Step 2: Update Google Calendar</h2>
          <p>Go to your Google Calendar.</p>
          <p>Find all sessions on and from your move date onwards.</p>
          <p>For each event:</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Right-click the event</li>
            <li>Change colour to <strong>Tomato (red)</strong> or <strong>Flamingo (pink)</strong></li>
            <li>When prompted, select <strong>“This event and following events”</strong></li>
          </ul>
          <p>
            This marks all future sessions as cancelled in the old system. But you will still be paid
            for all the previous sessions.
          </p>
        </section>

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel" data-step-panel>
          <h2>Step 3: Update Your Timesheet (Google Sheets)</h2>
          <p>Open your timesheet.</p>
          <p>Go to the correct month, for example <strong>April</strong>.</p>
          <p>Click <strong>“Sort Month”</strong>.</p>
          <p>
            This pulls in sessions from the <strong>1st</strong> up to the day before your move date.
          </p>
          <p>
            Example: If I moved to the new system on the <strong>18th</strong>, my timesheet would pull
            in all events <strong>1st–17th</strong>.
          </p>
        </section>

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel" data-step-panel>
          <h2>Step 4: Generate Invoice</h2>
          <p>You can generate and send your invoice mid way through the month.</p>
          <p>Share it with <strong>Dan</strong>.</p>
        </section>

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel" data-step-panel>
          <h2>Step 5: Start Using Salesforce</h2>
          <p>From your move date onwards:</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Set up your calendar in Salesforce as normal. This will be covered soon.</li>
          </ul>
        </section>

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel" data-step-panel>
          <h2>Important Rules</h2>
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Do not</strong> add past sessions into Salesforce</li>
            <li>Only add sessions from your move date onwards</li>
          </ul>
          <p>Example:</p>
          <p>Moving on <strong>18th</strong> → only add sessions from <strong>18th onwards</strong></p>
          <p><strong>Do not</strong> add anything from earlier in the month.</p>
        </section>

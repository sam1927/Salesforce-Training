      <div class="retro-content-kicker">Level 5</div>
      <h1>Availability</h1>
      <p class="retro-content-subtitle">How to set and update your availability and time slots so mentoring sessions can be booked with you.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="retro-stepper" data-stepper>

      <div class="retro-step-indicator">
        Step <span data-step-current>1</span> of <span data-step-total>8</span>
      </div>

      <div class="retro-step-stage">

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel is-active" data-step-panel>
          <h2>Overview</h2>
          <p>
            Once you have updated your <strong>games, equipment and preferences</strong>, the next step is to set your availability.
          </p>
        </section>

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel" data-step-panel>
          <h2>Set Your Availability (Operating Hours)</h2>
          <p>
            Before setting up your <a href="https://mindjam.my.site.com/Portal/s/operating-hours" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>availability</strong></a>, it helps to know why it matters.
          </p>
          <p>
            Your <strong>availability</strong> tells the <strong>system</strong> and the <strong>assigning team</strong> when you want to work.
          </p>
          <p>
            This helps them see which <strong>time slots</strong> are free when matching <strong>mentees</strong>.
          </p>
          <p>
            If you do not want to work on a particular <strong>day</strong> or at a particular <strong>time</strong>, simply do not add that time slot.
          </p>
          <p>
            Any time in your availability that is not already blocked by a <strong>session</strong> can appear as available for matching.
          </p>
        </section>

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel" data-step-panel>
          <h2>Example</h2>
          <p>
            If you usually work on Monday from <strong>9:00 to 5:00</strong>, but take a break from
            <strong>1:00 to 2:00</strong>, you would enter:
          </p>
          <ul>
            <li>9:00 to 1:00</li>
            <li>2:00 to 5:00</li>
          </ul>
          <p>
            This ensures <strong>1:00 to 2:00 does not appear as available.</strong>
          </p>

          <div class="retro-image-panel">
            <img
              src="/assets/images/availability/availabilityexample.gif"
              alt="Example of split availability time slots"
              class="retro-inline-image"
            >
          </div>
        </section>

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel" data-step-panel>
          <h2>How to Add Availability</h2>
          <ul>
            <li>Log in to the Community Site</li>
            <li>Go to <strong>Appointment Management</strong></li>
            <li>Open <strong>Manage Time Slots</strong></li>
            <li>Click the link with your name</li>
            <li>Click <strong>New</strong></li>
          </ul>

          <div class="retro-image-panel">
            <img
              src="/assets/images/availability/availabilityexample2.gif"
              alt="How to open a new availability time slot"
              class="retro-inline-image"
            >
          </div>

          <p>A new window will open. From here:</p>

          <ul>
            <li>Choose the day of the week</li>
            <li>Leave Type as <strong>Normal</strong></li>
            <li>Enter the start time</li>
            <li>Enter the end time</li>
            <li>Click <strong>Save</strong> or <strong>Save & New</strong></li>
          </ul>

          <p>
            If you are adding more than one time slot, <strong>Save & New</strong> is usually the easiest option.
          </p>
        </section>

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel" data-step-panel>
          <h2>Using the Example Above</h2>

          <p><strong>To add Monday 9:00 to 1:00:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Day of the Week: Monday</li>
            <li>Type: Normal</li>
            <li>Start Time: 9:00</li>
            <li>End Time: 1:00</li>
          </ul>
          <p>Then click <strong>Save & New</strong>.</p>

          <div class="retro-image-panel">
            <img
              src="/assets/images/availability/timeslot1.png"
              alt="Example of entering Monday 9:00 to 1:00 in a time slot"
              class="retro-inline-image"
            >
          </div>

          <p><strong>To add Monday 2:00 to 5:00:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Day of the Week: Monday</li>
            <li>Type: Normal</li>
            <li>Start Time: 2:00</li>
            <li>End Time: 5:00</li>
          </ul>
          <p>Then click <strong>Save</strong>.</p>

          <div class="retro-image-panel">
            <img
              src="/assets/images/availability/timeslot2.png"
              alt="Example of entering Monday 2:00 to 5:00 in a time slot"
              class="retro-inline-image"
            >
          </div>

          <p>
            Repeat this for any other days and times you want to add.
          </p>
        </section>

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel" data-step-panel>
          <h2>Updating Availability Later</h2>
          <p>
            If your availability changes, you can go back into <strong>Manage Time Slots</strong> and add new time slots at any time.
          </p>

          <p>To edit, delete, or clone a time slot:</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Find the day and time you want to change</li>
            <li>Click the small <strong>(▼)</strong> arrow icon next to it</li>
            <li>Choose <strong>Edit</strong>, <strong>Delete</strong>, or <strong>Clone</strong></li>
          </ul>
        </section>

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel" data-step-panel>
          <h2>Optional Alternative Method</h2>
          <p>
            There is another way to do this, but it is a little more clunky.
          </p>
          <p>Instead of entering separate working slots, you can:</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Add your full working day in Manage Time Slots</li>
            <li>Go to your calendar</li>
            <li>Add a new event for the time you do not want to work</li>
            <li>Set the event type to <strong>Personal Time</strong></li>
            <li>Set it to repeat weekly if needed</li>
          </ul>

          <p>For example, if you work 9:00 to 5:00 but want 1:00 to 2:00 blocked out, you could:</p>
          <ul>
            <li>add availability for 9:00 to 5:00</li>
            <li>then add a Personal Time event from 1:00 to 2:00</li>
          </ul>

          <p>
            This works because the blocked time will not appear as available.
          </p>

          <div class="retro-image-panel">
            <img
              src="/assets/images/availability/timeslot3.png"
              alt="Example of using a Personal Time event to block out time"
              class="retro-inline-image"
            >
          </div>

          <p>
            You do not need to call the event Lunch. You can call it something simple like <strong>Blocker</strong>.
          </p>
        </section>

        <section class="retro-panel retro-step-panel" data-step-panel>
          <h2>Important</h2>
          <p>
            If you use the calendar blocker method and forget to add the blocker, the system may treat that time as available.
          </p>
          <p>
            <strong>This could mean you appear free for times you did not want to offer.</strong>
          </p>
          <p>
            For most people, the clearest option is to enter availability as separate time slots.
          </p>
        </section>
